# Dimension Theorem

This folder proves the dimension theorem for Noetherian local rings in the form

```lean
ringKrullDim R = (growthDeg R : WithBot ℕ∞)
ringKrullDim R = (minGenPrimary R : WithBot ℕ∞)
```

Here `growthDeg R` is the least exponent bounding the Hilbert-Samuel function
`n ↦ length(R / maximalIdeal R ^ n)`, and `minGenPrimary R` is the least number
of generators of an ideal whose radical is the maximal ideal.

## File Guide

- `Numeric.lean` contains the purely numerical growth lemmas, especially the
  telescoping lemma used to lower the growth degree after quotienting by a
  nonzerodivisor.
- `Defs.lean` defines `colength`, `hilbertSamuel`, `growthDeg`, and
  `minGenPrimary`, and proves the basic finite-length and monotonicity facts.
- `GrowthLeDelta.lean` proves `growthDeg R ≤ minGenPrimary R` by monomial
  counting: an ideal generated by `k` elements gives an `O(n^k)` bound.
- `DimEqDelta.lean` proves `ringKrullDim R = minGenPrimary R` using Krull's
  height theorem and its converse from mathlib.
- `DimLeGrowth.lean` proves `ringKrullDim R ≤ growthDeg R` by induction on the
  growth bound. The inductive step reduces to a minimal-prime quotient, chooses
  a nonzero element of the maximal ideal in the resulting domain, uses
  Artin-Rees to compare Hilbert-Samuel functions, and applies the one-element
  dimension drop theorem.
- `Main.lean` assembles the inequalities into the final theorem.

## What Is Proved

The formal cycle is

```text
dim R ≤ growthDeg R ≤ minGenPrimary R ≤ dim R.
```

This is enough to identify the Krull dimension with the degree of polynomial
growth of the Hilbert-Samuel function.

## What Is Not Proved Here

The folder does not construct the Hilbert-Samuel polynomial, nor does it prove
that the Hilbert-Samuel function is eventually equal to a polynomial. Mathlib
does contain `Polynomial.hilbertPoly` for coefficients of rational functions
`p / (1 - X)^d`, but the graded-module Hilbert polynomial theorem needed to
apply it to associated graded rings is not part of this development.

So `growthDeg R` should be read as the formal replacement for "degree of the
Hilbert-Samuel polynomial" in this folder, not as a constructed polynomial.

## Sources, and how the formalization deviates from them

The skeleton is the classical proof of Atiyah-Macdonald, *Introduction to
Commutative Algebra*, Ch. 11 (Thm. 11.14); the same argument appears in Serre,
*Local Algebra*, in Matsumura, *Commutative Ring Theory* §13, and as Stacks
Project [tag 00KQ](https://stacks.math.columbia.edu/tag/00KQ). Deviations,
chosen to make the development self-contained over current Mathlib:

1. **`d(R)` is defined by growth order, not polynomial degree.** Classically
   `d(R)` is the degree of the Hilbert-Samuel polynomial, whose existence is
   the Hilbert-Serre theorem; this development avoids Hilbert-Serre entirely.
   The cost: the classical step "`P(n) - P(n-c)` has degree one less" is
   false for raw growth orders. The replacement is the telescoping lemma in
   `Numeric.lean` (`GrowthLE.of_le_sub`): for monotone `g` dominated by the
   `c`-step difference of `f`, summing along an arithmetic progression gives
   `n * g(n) ≤ f(n(c+1))`, forcing the degree drop. This lemma has no
   textbook counterpart.
2. **`d ≤ δ` by direct counting instead of graded machinery.**
   Atiyah-Macdonald deduce it from Hilbert-Serre applied to the associated
   graded ring `gr_q(R)` as a quotient of a polynomial ring; here the same
   idea is an explicit module surjection: `qⁿ/qⁿ⁺¹` is spanned over `R/q` by
   at most `(n+1)^(k-1)` monomial images.
3. **`δ ≤ dim` from Mathlib rather than prime avoidance.** The classical
   construction of a system of parameters is packaged inside Mathlib's
   converse to Krull's height theorem
   (`Ideal.exists_spanRank_eq_and_height_eq`,
   `Mathlib/RingTheory/Ideal/KrullsHeightTheorem.lean`, 2025); `DimEqDelta`
   only adds that the resulting ideal has radical `𝔪`.
4. **Chain bookkeeping via Mathlib's dimension-drop lemma.** The induction in
   `dim ≤ d` uses Mathlib's
   `ringKrullDim_quotient_span_singleton_succ_eq_ringKrullDim_of_mem_nonZeroDivisors`
   (`dim(R/(x)) + 1 = dim R` for a nonzerodivisor `x ∈ 𝔪`) instead of manual
   chain manipulation; the only hand-rolled chain transport is the reduction
   to `R/p` for `p` a minimal prime.

The Artin-Rees comparison of the Samuel functions of `R` and `R/(x)` in
`DimLeGrowth.lean` (via the colon-ideal bound `(𝔪ⁿ : x) ≤ 𝔪ⁿ⁻ᶜ`) follows the
textbook argument, using Mathlib's Artin-Rees lemma
`Ideal.exists_pow_inf_eq_pow_smul`.

## Relation to existing formalizations

- `dim = δ` alone is essentially a wrapper over Mathlib's 2025 height-theorem
  files; `dim = d` appears to be new relative to both Mathlib and the Guan-Hu
  depth / Auslander-Buchsbaum-Serre development
  ([arXiv:2510.24818](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24818)).
- Hilbert-Serre has a complete but stale proof in mathlib4 PR
  [#9819](https://github.com/leanprover-community/mathlib4/pull/9819)
  (J. Zhang, 2024) and in the `FMLJohn/HilbertPolynomial` repository; porting
  it would upgrade `growthDeg` to the honest Hilbert-Samuel polynomial degree.
